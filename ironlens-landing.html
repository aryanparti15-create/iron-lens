Research

Built on real
sports science.

Every IronLens recommendation is grounded in peer-reviewed research. We don't guess — we apply established principles from exercise science to your specific physique.

01
Mechanical tension drives hypertrophy
Schoenfeld (2010) established that mechanical tension is the primary driver of muscle growth — IronLens identifies which muscles aren't receiving adequate tension based on your visible development.
02
EMG-based exercise selection
Exercise recommendations are based on electromyography studies showing peak muscle activation — incline press for upper chest, cable laterals for lateral delts, and so on.
03
Leanness ≠ development
A key principle IronLens enforces — visible abs at 10% body fat does not mean developed abs. Muscle belly thickness is assessed independently from body fat percentage.
04
Progressive overload specificity
Workout recommendations follow progressive overload principles with rep ranges matched to the hypertrophy goal (6–12 reps for mass, 12–20 for metabolic stress).
